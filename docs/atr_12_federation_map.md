# ATR -- Federation & Dojo Map

A data-first map of major United States aikido federations: their governing structure, senior
teachers, lineage, scale, and representative dojos. It supports understanding the field's structure and prioritizing sources (Roadmap Phase 3) --
knowing which teachers anchor which collections and styles -- and is meant to be corrected and
extended over time.

**Scope and honesty.** Each federation has from dozens to 200+ affiliated dojos. Exhaustively
visiting every dojo's own site is not done here; that long tail is left to a live crawl
against each federation's official locator. What this captures is the durable structure --
governance, senior/shihan teachers, lineage, and a representative sample of named dojos --
which is what prioritization actually needs. Rosters change; this is a dated snapshot
(rev below), drawn from each federation's own site, to be refreshed against the source.

---

## 1. United States Aikido Federation (USAF)

- **Lineage.** Aikikai mainline. Founded by Yoshimitsu Yamada, Akira Tohei, Mitsunari Kanai,
  and Kazuo Chiba -- all direct students of Morihei Ueshiba. Official US representative to the
  International Aikido Federation; recognized by Aikikai Hombu. The oldest aikido organization
  in the US.
- **Naming caution.** "Akira Tohei" (Aikikai, Midwest) is a different person from "Koichi
  Tohei" (founder of the Ki Society, federation 2 below). Both were students of the founder;
  do not conflate them.
- **Regional history (explains the structure).** Incorporated 1976 and divided into regions,
  each led by a founder-student uchi-deshi: Eastern Region (Yamada and Kanai, New York /
  Boston), Midwest Region (Akira Tohei, Chicago), Western Region (Chiba, San Diego), with
  Sadao Yoshioka (Hawaii Aikikai) also among the early Shihankai (formed 1982). Seiichi Sugano
  joined Yamada in New York in 1987. In 2005 the USAF centralized into one organization; at
  that point Akira Tohei's Midwest Aikido Federation continued separately, and Chiba's group
  became Birankai International.
- **Governance.** Since Yamada Shihan's death (Jan 2023), under a Technical Committee plus a
  Board of Directors. TC membership requires minimum 6th dan Shihan; 4-year renewable terms.
- **Scale.** 200+ Aikikai-affiliated member dojos; ~25 Shihan in North America.
- **Technical Committee (current, named, with home dojos).**
  - Peter Bernath (Chair) -- Florida Aikikai
  - Penny Bernath -- Florida Aikikai
  - Harvey Konigsberg -- Woodstock Aikido
  - Andy Demko -- Aikido of New Castle
  - Steve Pimsler -- New York Aikikai
  - Maria Cristina Dumlao -- Gold Coast Aikikai
  - Vu Ha -- Boston Aikikai
  - Blue Spruell -- Peachtree Aikikai (Atlanta)
- **Founding/senior Shihan (historical, many in memoriam).** Yoshimitsu Yamada (New York
  Aikikai), Mitsunari Kanai (New England Aikikai), Akira Tohei (Midwest Aikido Center), Kazuo
  Chiba (San Diego), Seiichi Sugano (New York Aikikai), plus a broad Shihan roster (e.g.
  Ariff Mehter, Aikido of Cincinnati; Larry Bieri, Finger Lakes Aikido; Hal Lehrman, Aikido of
  Park Slope).
- **Anchor dojos.** New York Aikikai (the historical hub), Florida Aikikai, Boston Aikikai,
  Woodstock Aikido, Peachtree Aikikai, Gold Coast Aikikai.
- **Source.** usaikifed.com (dojo locator at services.usaikifed.com/dojos).

## 1a. Midwest Aikido Federation / Midwest Aikido Center (MAF / MAC)

- **Relationship.** Originally the USAF Midwest Region; continued as its own organization after
  the USAF's 2005 centralization. Closely related to the USAF by shared Aikikai lineage and
  history; listed separately because it now operates independently.
- **Lineage.** Aikikai. Founded by Akira Tohei Shihan (1929-1999), 8th dan, dispatched from
  Hombu to Chicago in 1972; he established the Midwest Aikido Federation (1972) and the Midwest
  Aikido Center (1975), and chaired both the USAF Technical Committee and the North American
  Shihankai. Under his guidance the MAF grew to 50+ dojos across the Midwest.
- **Scale.** 50+ dojos across the Midwest (at its peak under Tohei).
- **Anchor dojo.** Midwest Aikido Center, Chicago (50th anniversary in 2025).
- **Note.** Akira Tohei died in 1999; the MAC continues. Confirm current chief instructor and
  the present MAF roster against the MAC site (midwestaikidocenter.org).
- **Source.** midwestaikidocenter.org; usaikifed.com/midwestaikidocenter.

## 2. Ki Society (Shinshin Toitsu Aikido / Ki-Aikido)

- **Lineage.** Founded by Koichi Tohei (1971), who split from the Aikikai in 1974. Emphasis on
  ki principles and mind-body coordination -- a distinct style and curriculum.
- **Governance.** Headquarters in Tochigi, Japan, under Shinichi Tohei (President / Kaicho,
  son of and successor to Koichi Tohei). US teaching quality overseen from HQ Japan.
- **US structure.** Organized as individual dojos, small groups, and four larger Federations,
  each under a Chief Instructor:
  - Midland Ki Federation -- Koichi Kashiwaya (Boulder, CO; Rocky Mountain Ki Society);
    ~25 member schools across USA, Brazil, Canada.
  - Hawaii Ki Federation
  - Northern California Ki Society
  - (a fourth regional federation; confirm name against HQ list)
- **Scale.** 50,000+ practitioners worldwide; US member dojos across the four federations.
- **Anchor dojos.** Rocky Mountain Ki Society (Boulder), regional federation HQs.
- **Source.** Ki Aikido USA / ki-society.com (nation/usa).

## 3. Aikido Association of America (AAA) / Aikido Association International (AAI)

- **Lineage.** Founded by Fumio Toyoda Shihan (AAA 1984, AAI 1986). Toyoda trained in the Ki
  Society and Aikikai lines; AAA/AAI re-affiliated with Aikikai Hombu in 1994. Strong
  associated Rinzai Zen training tradition.
- **Governance.** Headquartered in Chicago. Founder/Chief Instructor was Toyoda (d. 2001);
  instruction codified in an instructor's manual and seminar/certification program. After
  Toyoda's death, Andrew Sato served as Chief Instructor, then parted ways in 2004.
- **Scale.** 140+ affiliated facilities in the US (AAA); AAI affiliated dojo in 10+ nations.
- **Lineage note (splits).** Several successor groups trace to Toyoda: the Aikido World
  Alliance (AWA, Andrew Sato, 2005, 50+ dojo), Zenshinkai, and others. Relevant to mapping
  since these share the Toyoda technical base.
- **Anchor dojos.** Tenshinkan Dojo (Chicago, HQ), and the AAA dojo locator.
- **Source.** aaa-aikido.com (US and international dojo locators).

## 4. Shin Kaze Aikido Alliance

- **Lineage.** Aikikai. Formed in the tradition of the senior Aikikai shihan who taught in
  North America; its stated "family tree" of teachers includes N. Tamura, A. Tohei, S. Sugano,
  K. Chiba, Y. Yamada, Y. Kawahara, and Y. Kurita. Rooted partly in Kanai Sensei's planned
  "Kaze no Kai," which his death in 2004 halted.
- **Governance.** Non-profit registered in Canada (April 2019); HQ in Toronto. Board of
  Directors plus a Technical Committee of North America-resident Shihan. Registers and
  standardizes gradings per Hombu international regulations. Emphasizes freedom (dojos invite
  whom they wish) and participatory, two-way governance.
- **Founding Technical Committee / senior teachers (with dojos).**
  - Barbara Britton -- Framingham Aikikai (senior instructor)
  - David Farrell -- Commonwealth Aikikai (chief instructor)
  - David Halprin -- Framingham Aikikai and Providence Aikikai (chief instructor)
  - Robert Zimmermann -- Toronto Aikikai (chief instructor; founding member)
  - Josef Birdsong -- Aikido of Austin (added to TC, Sept 2020)
- **Scale.** Smaller, newer alliance; dozens of member dojos across North America.
- **Anchor dojos.** Toronto Aikikai (HQ), Framingham Aikikai, Commonwealth Aikikai,
  Providence Aikikai, Aikido of Austin.
- **Source.** shinkazeaikidoalliance.com.

## Cross-federation notes

- **Shared Aikikai root.** USAF, the Midwest Aikido Federation, and Shin Kaze are all
  Aikikai-line and share teacher heritage (Yamada, A. Tohei, Chiba, Kanai, Tamura, Sugano);
  Shin Kaze partly formed from teachers and dojos connected to that same group, and the
  Midwest body was originally the USAF Midwest Region. Expect overlap and movement of dojos
  between Aikikai-recognized bodies.
- **Distinct styles.** Ki Society (Tohei) and the Toyoda line (AAA/AAI and its successors) are
  technically distinct from the Aikikai mainline and from each other; the taxonomy and
  technique emphasis differ, which matters for cross-style study (Roadmap, research use).
- **For prioritization.** The named senior teachers above anchor the major lines; the anchor
  dojos are the most established collections in each body.

## Open items

- **Full dojo rosters.** Each federation's complete, current dojo list lives behind a locator
  on its own site and changes often; pull live against the official source rather than relying
  on this snapshot. This map captures structure and seniors, not the full membership.
- **Per-dojo instructors.** Individual dojo sites list chief instructors and assistants
  (shidoin / fukushidoin); these were not exhaustively visited. A targeted per-dojo pass is a
  separate task.
- **Other US bodies not mapped here.** ASU (Aikido Schools of Ueshiba, Saotome line),
  Yoshinkan groups, Tomiki/Shodokan, Seidokan, and independent dojos are outside this set of
  four but exist and may matter later.
- **Verification.** Names, ranks, and roles are a dated snapshot from federation sites; confirm
  against the source before relying on them, and treat all as subject to teacher correction.
